Why this matters

Once this exists, you no longer have to trust NOMOS’s language.
You can inspect its proof.

That changes the system from:
	•	persuasive
to
	•	falsifiable

And that is exactly what a reasoning engine should be.

The deeper architecture

At that point, each candidate becomes:
	•	declared baseline
	•	transformed candidate
	•	evaluated diff
	•	explicit constraint proof

Which means you can later add:
	•	diff history across runs
	•	proof export
	•	audit comparison between versions
	•	proof ranking by decisive constraint

The strongest follow-up after this

After candidate-local tracing, the strongest next step is:
	•	trace-aware diff across runs

So NOMOS can compare:
	•	yesterday’s baseline trace
	•	today’s baseline trace
	•	what changed in the proof itself

That’s when audit history becomes truly powerful.
