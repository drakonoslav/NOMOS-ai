Build governance decision support for NOMOS.

Goal:
Help the human compare the current active policy against the recommended policy before promotion or rollback.

This layer is advisory only.
It must not execute governance changes automatically.

Create:
- src/audit/governance_decision_support_types.ts
- src/audit/governance_decision_support.ts
- src/ui/components/audit/GovernanceDecisionSupportPanel.tsx

Requirements:

1. Add canonical decision-support types.

interface GovernanceDecisionSupport {
  domain: "nutrition" | "training" | "schedule" | "generic";

  currentActivePolicyVersionId: string | null;
  recommendedPolicyVersionId: string | null;

  expectedGains: string[];
  expectedTradeoffs: string[];
  expectedRisks: string[];

  recommendationStrength: "weak" | "moderate" | "strong";
  confidence: "low" | "moderate" | "high";

  promoteSuggested: boolean;
  rollbackSuggested: boolean;

  summaryLines: string[];
}

2. Build deterministic functions:
- buildGovernanceDecisionSupport(currentPolicy, recommendedPolicy, benchReport, recommendationReport)
- computeExpectedGains(...)
- computeExpectedTradeoffs(...)
- computeExpectedRisks(...)

3. Decision-support rules:
- compare active policy vs recommended policy on:
  - exact-match rate
  - direction-match rate
  - too-aggressive rate
  - too-weak rate
  - unresolved rate
- expected gains should reflect likely measurable improvement
- expected tradeoffs should reflect likely measurable cost
- expected risks should reflect uncertainty, shallow evidence, or mixed bench results
- if evidence is weak or too close, promoteSuggested must remain false

4. Example expected gains:
- "Exact-match performance is likely to improve."
- "Over-aggressive forecasts are likely to decrease."
- "Unresolved prediction rate may decline."

5. Example expected tradeoffs:
- "Confidence outputs may become slightly more conservative."
- "Direction-match gain may come with lower escalation sensitivity."

6. Example expected risks:
- "Bench evidence is limited to a shallow nutrition window."
- "Policy differences are modest; practical gains may be small."

7. UI:
Create GovernanceDecisionSupportPanel showing:
- current active policy
- recommended policy
- expected gains
- expected tradeoffs
- expected risks
- recommendation strength
- confidence
- suggestion flags

8. Keep everything deterministic.
9. Do not trigger promotion or rollback here.
10. This panel must remain separate from the actual governance action buttons.

Success condition:
NOMOS can help a human understand whether promoting or rolling back a policy is justified before any governance action is taken.
