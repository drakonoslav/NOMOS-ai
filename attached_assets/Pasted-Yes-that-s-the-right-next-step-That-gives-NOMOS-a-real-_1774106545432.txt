Why this matters

This is what lets you answer:
	•	Did v3 really improve anything?
	•	Or does it just sound more cautious?
	•	Does policy change alter:
	•	predicted variable
	•	confidence
	•	risk direction
	•	explanation

That’s much stronger than just comparing aggregate metrics.

What the output should look like

For one nutrition run:
	•	Policy v2
	•	predicted variable: calorie delta
	•	confidence: moderate
	•	risk direction: rising
	•	Policy v3
	•	predicted variable: calorie delta
	•	confidence: low
	•	risk direction: stable

Difference summary
	•	confidence changed from moderate to low
	•	risk direction changed from rising to stable
	•	predicted variable unchanged

That is extremely useful.

The key discipline

Replay should vary policy only.

Not:
	•	different input
	•	different canonical declaration
	•	different hidden context

Otherwise the comparison is contaminated.
