The correct parser idea

You need a layer like:

relation_binder.ts

It should take extracted quantified entities and bind them into relations.

Canonical idea:

interface MeasuredEntity {
  id: string;
  label: string;
  amount: number | null;
  unit: string | null;
}

interface RelationBinding {
  id: string;
  subjectId: string;
  relation:
    | "before"
    | "after"
    | "within"
    | "during"
    | "until"
    | "since"
    | "between"
    | "around"
    | "near"
    | "inside"
    | "outside"
    | "under"
    | "over";

  objectId: string | null;

  offsetAmount?: number | null;
  offsetUnit?: string | null;

  rawText: string;
}

Example:

30 minutes before lifting

becomes a RelationBinding:
	•	subject: some intake event
	•	relation: before
	•	object: lifting
	•	offsetAmount: 30
	•	offsetUnit: minute

The deep principle

You are not really building:
	•	a food parser
	•	a time parser
	•	a workout parser

You are building:

a measurable-entity + relation parser

That is much more powerful.

Because then NOMOS can understand all of these with the same machinery:
	•	80g dextrin 30 minutes before lifting
	•	5mg melatonin 2 hours before bed
	•	3 miles after work
	•	2 capsules magnesium with dinner
	•	40 lb dumbbell for 12 reps
	•	2 months after promotion
	•	1 decade before the merger
	•	3 meters from the wall

Same grammar spine.

⸻

One important caution

Do not try to make a literally complete “master list of all nouns.”

That will fail.

Do make:
	•	a strong unit registry
	•	a strong relation lexicon
	•	a modest anchor vocabulary
	•	open-vocabulary noun capture

So the closed lists are for:
	•	units
	•	relations
	•	common anchors

Not for all possible objects.

The best next abstraction after this

After this, the strongest next step is:

operand graph / relation graph

So NOMOS can store:
	•	entity A measured in g
	•	entity B measured in minutes
	•	A occurs before anchor C by B
	•	constraint applies to A-class entities inside a given window

That is where parsing becomes true symbolic reasoning.
