What this actually gives you

Right now, your system has:

⸻

What it should feel like

A good deliberation summary should read like:

Recommendation: Promote v3 (moderate strength, high confidence)

Evidence
	•	v3 improves exact-match rate vs v2
	•	No increase in unresolved outcomes
	•	Aggressiveness reduced

Gains
	•	Better exact-match stability
	•	Lower over-aggressive corrections

Tradeoffs
	•	Slight reduction in direction-match rate

Risks
	•	Limited evaluation window (5 runs)

Supporting doctrines
	•	Prefer conservative promotion under elevated unresolved rate

Caution doctrines
	•	Avoid strong promotion under shallow history

Synthesis
	•	Evidence supports promotion with acceptable tradeoffs
	•	Doctrine alignment is positive but limited by shallow history

Decision
→ Promote v3 or hold for more evidence?

⸻

The key design principle

This layer must:
	•	compress → not expand
	•	clarify → not reinterpret
	•	present tension → not resolve it automatically

If it ever feels like it’s deciding instead of informing, it’s wrong.
